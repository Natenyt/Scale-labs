What problem are we solving?
In Uzbekistan, major industries — especially banks, telecommunication companies, e-commerce platforms like Uzum and Yandex Market, and logistics services — rely heavily on constant communication with their customers.

Every day, these businesses must:

Update customers about payments
Remind them of overdue loans
Confirm appointments
Verify identities
Send delivery updates
Answer thousands of repetitive questions
To handle this, companies hire large teams of call center operators, often running 24/7 support, divided into 3 shifts per day.

This creates three huge problems:

1. Extremely High Operational Costs
To work 24 hours, a company needs three operators per seat. If an average operator earns 6 million UZS, this becomes:

18 million UZS per month for one position

216 million UZS per year

Most large companies have dozens — sometimes hundreds — of such positions. This is one of the biggest hidden expenses in Uzbekistan’s service sector.

2. Slow and Inconsistent Customer Service
Human agents:

get tired
make mistakes
provide inconsistent information
cannot scale during peak hours
A sudden spike in calls can overwhelm the system instantly.

3. Low Efficiency and Repetitive Tasks
Over 70% of operator work is repetitive:

“Your loan payment is due”
“Your courier will arrive today”
“Your balance is…”
“Your order is being prepared”
Repetition wastes human time, slows companies down, and creates bottlenecks.

Why This Problem Matters
These inefficiencies directly affect:

customer satisfaction
company profit
overall digital transformation in Uzbekistan
And as communication demands grow year after year, this problem becomes even more expensive and unavoidable.

How i plan to solve it
Approach
My core approach is to build modular AI workflows. These workflows define how an agent behaves inside a specific industry scenario, including rules, decision paths, knowledge boundaries, and integration logic.

The agent itself is powered by the core model cluster, but the workflow determines its personality, responsibilities, and functional behavior. This allows me to support multiple industries without building separate products:

Telecom Workflow
Customer support, billing issues, SIM activation, complaints, prepaid packages.

Banking Workflow
Loan reminders, customer info updates, branch routing, account FAQs.

Logistics Workflow
Shipment updates, warehouse communication, scheduling.

Clients don’t “install” anything. They create an agent on my website, choose a workflow (or request a custom one), provide API keys/data schema, and the agent starts operating autonomously.

Result: An agent that enters the organization’s workflow instantly.

How I Use AI
1. Speech Recognition (STT)
Converts caller audio into text using different engines for different languages (11 Labs for English, Yandex for Uzbek).

2. Reasoning & Decision-Making
The ChatGPT cluster handles intent understanding, rule following, data gathering, and autonomous decision-making.

3. Tool Calling & Integration
The LLM calls tools to update info, check bills, fetch statuses, create tickets, and execute workflow tasks.

4. Text-to-Speech (TTS)
Converts final responses to natural, human-like speech with emotion and tone.

5. Agent Orchestration
Ensures real-time streaming, low latency, error recovery, and workflow-aware decisions.
